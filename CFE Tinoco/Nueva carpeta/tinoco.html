Why is it a natural monopoly?

Building a national electrical grid requires billions of pesos in infrastructure. It is economically unfeasible for more than one company to build parallel networks — the fixed costs are so high that a single company can serve the entire market more cheaply than two.

Barriers to Entry

1) Massive capital required (irreplicable infrastructure)
2) Legal framework: CFE is a State company with exclusive concession
3) Economies of scale impossible to match
4) Distribution network covering 99.7% of national territory

Degree of Pricing Power

CFE is a price maker: it sets the rates. The government regulates them to protect consumers, since without regulation CFE could charge prices well above marginal cost.

03 — Public Utility Monopoly

Why is this a Public Utility Monopoly?

🏛️
Public Utility Monopoly

Electricity is an essential good whose provision through a single network operator is more efficient than duplicating infrastructure. The state maintains control of transmission and distribution networks to guarantee universal access, affordable prices, and national energy security.

⚙️
Efficient Infrastructure

This is the classic case of a regulated natural monopoly: having a single provider minimizes infrastructure costs, while regulation prevents abuse of dominant position.

03 — Microeconomic Analysis

DEMAND & CONSUMER BEHAVIOR

Price Elasticity

INELASTIC DEMAND

Electricity is a basic necessity: even if the price rises, consumers cannot easily reduce their consumption or switch providers. Price elasticity is close to 0 in the short term. A 10% increase in rates does not reduce consumption proportionally — people still need light and refrigeration.

Competition Level

COMPETITION: NONE

In the residential segment, there is no competition. Since the 2014 energy reform, private companies can generate electricity, but distribution and commercialization to end users remains exclusive to CFE. There is no competitive pressure to lower prices or improve quality.

Consumer Behavior

CAPTIVE CONSUMER

The consumer does not maximize utility by choosing between providers — there is no choice. The only decision is how much to consume at the regulated price. There is no search for alternatives or brand comparison. The only way out is to generate their own energy (solar panels), an expensive alternative accessible only to some.

Demand Conditions

STABLE AND GROWING DEMAND

Electricity demand grows with population and economic development. It does not depend on trends or fashions. It is predictable, constant and essential. Factors like climate generate seasonal peaks, but base demand never drops to zero — this gives CFE an extremely stable revenue stream.

04 — Marketing Strategy

POSITIONING

Pricing Strategy

CFE does not compete on price — it sets it. Rates are regulated by the government and differentiated by user type: residential, high-consumption residential (DAC), commercial and industrial. Subsidies to the residential sector allow low prices that reinforce service dependence.

Promotion

Being a monopoly, CFE does not need advertising to attract customers. Its marketing focuses on institutional image and sustainability (CFE Green, clean energy). The most powerful contact point is the bimonthly bill — it reaches 100% of its market at no advertising cost.

Positioning

CFE positions itself as a strategic national company, pride of Mexico. It does not seek to be "preferred" — it seeks to be perceived as reliable and essential. It reinforces its image with the energy sovereignty narrative: "energy belongs to Mexicans".
